# Raptric Website Redesign — Brief v2 (supersedes v1)

> **Status note:** This replaces the original "operating system builder" brief entirely (ladder framework, LinkOS/GuestSquad platform positioning, Capabilities/Platforms nav). That direction was built, reviewed, and discarded by the client on 2026-07-02. Do not resurrect it. This document is now the sole authority for content/positioning decisions.

## Project Goal

Redesign raptric.com into a modern, lean, high-trust website that presents Raptric as:

- an automation-first company
- an AI specialist
- a team with deep technical and operational expertise

The new site should feel like a modern technology company, not a generic services website. The goal is not to show everything — it's to make the right buyer think: *these people understand automation, systems, and execution.*

## What Stays

Business concept centered on five service areas, which are also the top navigation:

1. AI Automation
2. SaaS Development
3. Customer Operations
4. Business Operations
5. Technology Advisory

## Final Top Navigation

`AI Automation · SaaS Development · Customer Operations · Business Operations · Technology Advisory`

Header also includes: Raptric logo (left), **Let's Talk** CTA (right). No extra top-level items unless necessary. About, Insights, Privacy Policy, and Terms live in the footer or a secondary menu.

## What's Wrong With the Current Site (fix these)

- Feels dated and template-driven
- Homepage too busy, too broad
- Messaging fragmented and unclear
- Service presentation reads like a generic agency/outsourcing company
- Weak visual hierarchy
- Doesn't communicate deep AI/systems/automation expertise
- Too many dropdown-style service ideas reduce trust instead of increasing it

## Positioning

**Raptric is:** an AI automation and systems specialist with deep expertise in software, operations, and execution — modern, technical, lean, precise, premium, automation-first, systems-focused, execution-driven.

**Raptric is not:** a digital marketing agency, a generic outsourcing company, a broad software house, a cluttered "we do everything" services firm.

## Design Direction

Feel: modern, premium, calm, technical, clear, future-forward, product-company level, high-trust. Closer to AI SaaS companies, workflow automation platforms, modern B2B product brands, technical product studios. Not a WordPress services template, call center company, freelancer marketplace, or a slide deck turned into a website.

**Visual language:** strong grid-based layout, large typography, minimal but intentional color, premium whitespace, subtle dividers, layered product-style composition, custom system graphics, interface-inspired visuals, abstract diagrams showing flow/automation/orchestration/systems. Custom AI/system illustrations, dashboard-like cards, workflow visuals, automation signals, operating-system-style graphic motifs, subtle visual layering.

**Avoid:** stock office photos, headset imagery, handshake imagery, "team meeting" stock scenes, random robots, generic icons everywhere, carousel sliders, over-animated gimmicks.

## Homepage Strategy

Lean. Do not fully explain every service on the homepage. Position Raptric clearly, establish trust, introduce the five core areas, show why Raptric is different, drive visitors deeper or into contact. Not a service encyclopedia, giant SEO page, or long repetitive sales document. **6–7 sections max:**

1. Hero
2. What Raptric Does
3. Core Areas
4. Why Raptric
5. Problems We Solve
6. How We Work
7. Let's Talk

## Hero Requirements

Most important part of the redesign. Must immediately communicate: Raptric specializes in AI automation and systems; Raptric also builds the software and operational structures around that; Raptric is a serious technical partner.

Include: one strong headline, one short supporting paragraph, one primary CTA, one secondary CTA, a premium right-side visual composition.

Headline direction: *"Automate operations. Build better systems."* (alt: *"AI automation and systems built for real business execution."*)

Support copy direction: *"Raptric helps businesses streamline operations, improve execution, and build scalable systems through AI automation, SaaS development, and structured operational support."*

CTA options: Let's Talk / Start a Conversation / Explore AI Automation

## Core Areas (exact copy basis)

- **AI Automation** — AI workflows, AI agents, internal assistants, process automation, business systems
- **SaaS Development** — Custom SaaS platforms, internal products, dashboards, portals, APIs, integrations
- **Customer Operations** — Support systems, call center operations, service workflows, onboarding, communication processes
- **Business Operations** — VA-enabled workflows, admin systems, reporting, CRM operations, process execution
- **Technology Advisory** — Systems planning, architecture, automation strategy, technical guidance

Each appears on the homepage as a clean card / high-level summary block with a short description and a path into its dedicated page — a strategic capability, not a random service list item.

## Branding Angle

Communicate deep expertise, serious execution capability, clarity of thought, modern AI relevance, operational maturity. The brand should not feel loud — it should feel sharp.

## SEO Approach

Homepage: broad category relevance, internal links, strong semantic structure, conversion — not keyword-overloaded. Service pages: detail, search targeting, use cases, problem/solution content, FAQs, long-form explanation.

## Page Architecture

Each nav item is its own landing page: AI Automation, SaaS Development, Customer Operations, Business Operations, Technology Advisory. Each includes: clear H1, short intro, what this area covers, business problems solved, example workflows/use cases, why Raptric is credible here, CTA.

## Tone of Copy

Write like a technical operator, a systems thinker, a founder, a modern product company. Short paragraphs, clear language, strong headlines, confident simplicity. Avoid hype, filler, "best in class," "cutting-edge solutions," "one-stop shop," "innovative digital agency," exaggerated claims.

## Standing Hard Rules (carried over from v1 — still binding regardless of positioning)

- Never mention DentOS or TransData anywhere, in any form, in any context
- No invented stats, client names, testimonials, or logos
- No pricing/tiers unless explicitly requested later

## Success Criteria

- Immediately feels more modern than the current live version
- Raptric clearly reads as AI-automation-and-systems-focused
- Homepage is easy to understand
- Five core areas clearly organized
- Feels premium and specialized, not generic-services
- Visitors quickly understand what Raptric does and why it matters

## Immediate Build Scope

Redesigned homepage, new header/footer, reusable component system, five service landing page templates, modern visual system aligned to AI automation/systems thinking, custom hero graphics/illustration direction, responsive layouts (desktop/tablet/mobile).
