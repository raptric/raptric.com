# Raptric Master Build Brief

Date: July 4, 2026  
Purpose: single source of truth for rebuilding Raptric around positioning, SEO, lead generation, navigation, and page-by-page content direction

## 1. Executive Summary

Raptric should be built and marketed as:

`An AI-enhanced operations, support, and engineering partner for growing companies.`

The new site should not feel like:

- a generic digital agency
- a traditional BPO
- a random software house
- a service catalog with unrelated offers

The strongest market position is:

- AI automation
- support operations
- engineering teams

That gives Raptric a clean story:

- automate work
- run operations better
- add engineering capacity when needed

## 2. What We Learned From The Audit

## Old site issues

The previous Raptric website had major SEO and quality problems:

- one repeated title tag across many pages
- missing standard meta descriptions
- inconsistent sitemap setup
- indexable low-value/template URLs
- thin and generic service copy
- placeholder text and typos
- weak trust signals
- scattered positioning

## New live site strengths

The new live build is already much better in:

- design quality
- positioning clarity
- message consistency
- visual trust
- strategic framing

Strong live positioning example:

- `We build operating systems for business workflows.`

## New live site issues to fix

These still need attention:

- `robots.txt` returns 404
- `sitemap.xml` returns 404
- `/about` returns 404
- canonical tags were not detected on sampled pages
- Open Graph tags were not detected on sampled pages
- JSON-LD schema was not detected on sampled pages
- `/contact` appears semantically thin and lacked a detected `H1`
- live nav still does not match the final structure

## 3. Core Positioning

## Brand thesis

Raptric helps companies build operating systems for growth by combining:

- AI automation
- support operations
- engineering execution

## Best positioning line

Recommended umbrella positioning:

`Raptric helps companies scale support, automate operations, and ship products faster with AI workflows, human teams, and embedded engineering capacity.`

## What Raptric should become known for

Raptric should own this mental category:

`AI automation + human support + engineering capacity`

## 4. Final Navigation Structure

This should become the primary site structure.

## Final top navigation

- AI Automation
- Support Operations
- Engineering Team
- Solutions
- About
- Contact

## Why this nav works

- `AI Automation` captures the modern automation and AI systems layer
- `Support Operations` is clearer and more commercial than `Customer Operations`
- `Engineering Team` is clearer and more valuable than vague advisory language
- `Solutions` lets us group use cases, business problems, and industry/application pages
- `About` and `Contact` strengthen trust and conversion

## What should leave the primary nav

These labels are weaker and should not remain as primary top-level nav items:

- Business Operations
- Technology Advisory
- generic SaaS-only top-level emphasis

SaaS belongs under `Engineering Team`.

## 8. Messaging Rules

## What to say

- AI plus human operations
- systems, not isolated tasks
- build plus operational accountability
- automation tied to real workflows
- support that scales without quality collapse
- engineering capacity embedded into business execution

## What not to say

- best digital marketing agency
- we do everything
- vague transformation language
- generic BPO claims
- pure advisory positioning without delivery proof

## Best messaging pattern

Raptric should repeatedly communicate:

- We build the workflows
- We support the operation
- We provide the engineering capacity

## 9. Technical SEO Requirements

These should be treated as required before broader SEO rollout.

- publish working `robots.txt`
- publish working `sitemap.xml`
- fix `/about` 404
- add canonical tags
- add Open Graph tags
- add JSON-LD schema
- ensure every page has one clear `H1`
- create metadata per page
- use consistent URL structure

## Metadata requirements per page

Every indexable page should have:

- unique title tag
- unique meta description
- canonical URL
- Open Graph title
- Open Graph description
- Open Graph image

## Minimum schema

- Organization
- WebSite
- BreadcrumbList
- Article on insight posts
- Service where appropriate

## 10. Content Requirements Per Service Page

Every commercial page should include:

- one clear primary keyword theme
- one strong `H1`
- above-the-fold explanation of what Raptric does
- problems solved
- what the service covers
- how Raptric works
- use cases
- CTA
- internal links to related services
- FAQ section where useful
- proof elements when available

Build Raptric around this market position:

`AI automation + support operations + engineering teams`

Use the final nav structure as the organizing system:

- AI Automation
- Support Operations
- Engineering Team
- Solutions
- About
- Contact

Then improve page by page using this rule:
